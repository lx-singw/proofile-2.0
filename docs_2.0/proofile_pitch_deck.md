## Slide 3 — The Problem

### Three problems. One root cause.

**The root cause:** Professional reputation is self-reported, isolated, and unverifiable.

---

**Problem 1 — CVs Are Fiction**
Studies estimate 30%+ of CVs in South Africa contain false information. Fabricated degrees. Inflated titles. Reference contacts who are friends, not former managers. Employers know this — and still cannot systematically solve it.

**Problem 2 — The Hidden Job Market**
70–80% of jobs are filled through networks and referrals. Talented people without the right connections are structurally locked out — not because they lack skills, but because they lack access to the trust graph. The graph exists. It's just invisible.

**Problem 3 — Reputation is Fragmented and Non-Portable**
You have a rating on Upwork. Stars on Airbnb. GitHub contributions. LinkedIn endorsements. A verbal reference from a manager who thinks you're exceptional. None of it is connected. None of it travels with you. Every platform starts from zero trust. This is why fraud is a trillion-dollar global problem.

## Appendix A — POPIA & Legal Compliance

South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) governs all personal data on the platform.

**Key commitments:**
- Explicit informed consent at signup for all data categories
- Public reviews are attributed — the reviewer's name is always shown (accountability mechanism)
- Users have full right to access, correct, and delete their data
- API data is exclusively aggregated and anonymised — no individual profiles shared via API without explicit user consent
- A designated POPIA Information Officer is appointed from launch
- Full legal review by SA data privacy counsel before launch
- Employment law counsel review of review/defamation policy

## Appendix B — Key Questions & Answers

**"Won't managers refuse to leave public reviews — too much liability?"**
We make it opt-in and clearly voluntary. The review form includes a declaration. Legal language is plain and protective. Early evidence: managers already write LinkedIn recommendations (public) and take reference calls (verbal with no protection). Proofile is safer than both — written, consensual, disputable.

**"What about defamatory reviews?"**
Our dispute mechanism allows any reviewed party to flag a review. Flagged reviews are paused pending investigation. We maintain legal counsel specifically for this. The fact that the reviewer's name is always attached dramatically reduces malicious review risk — anonymity is what enables review abuse on other platforms.

**"Can't LinkedIn just build this?"**
LinkedIn is a US company with a $26B acquisition price and a core product that is fundamentally self-reported. Pivoting to verified-only reviews would invalidate 90% of their existing content and alienate their user base. They are structurally unable to do what Proofile does. Google couldn't kill Reddit. Facebook couldn't kill WhatsApp. Purpose-built wins over general-purpose in trust-sensitive categories.

**"Why won't someone copy this immediately?"**
The graph is the moat, not the interface. Copying the interface is trivial. Replicating 30,000 verified review edges and the trust signals they encode is not. Every day we run, our data advantage compounds. Speed of execution is the entire strategy.
